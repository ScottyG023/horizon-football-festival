About Horizon

More than a tournament.
A weekend on the Northern Beaches.

Cromer Park sits at the edge of one of Australia's great coastal stretches. Here's why the Horizon Football Festival is built for a long weekend, not just a tournament.

Stop 01 · The Pitch

Cromer Park, from above.

Eight full-size synthetic pitches, 30 minutes from Sydney CBD. Home of Manly United and one of NSW's premier football venues. Floodlights, a clubhouse, ample parking, and the ocean fifteen minutes east.

Cromer Park · 8 pitches · 30 min from CBD
Stop 02 · The Game

Real tournament football. For real young footballers.

Group stage on day one, Bronze/Silver/Gold knockouts on day two. Every team plays a minimum of ten matches. Designed by coaches, for coaches — with developmental rhythm built into the schedule, not bolted on.

7-a-side · 18-minute games · 540+ matches across the festival
Stop 03 · The Town

Manly, fifteen minutes east.

Between matches, the Northern Beaches are right there. Manly is fifteen minutes from the pitch — the ferry to Circular Quay, the surf clubs, the long beach walk to Shelly. Dee Why, Curl Curl, Freshwater within easy reach.

Manly Beach · 15 min from Cromer Park
Stop 04 · The Trip

Make a weekend of it.

Travelling from interstate or overseas? Cromer Park is 30 minutes from Sydney CBD and surrounded by accommodation, restaurants and beaches. Sydney Harbour and the Manly ferry are part of the offer — football by day, the city by afternoon.

Manly ferry · Circular Quay → Manly Wharf · 18 min
